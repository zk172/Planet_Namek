An Introduction:
This section is designed to give you a good idea of the relative power levels that Dragon Ball characters have as they do battle from the beginning of Dragon Ball all the way through the end of Dragon Ball Z. There are two basic types of power levels on this list.

1) Power Levels which we are reporting directly from the Dragon Ball Manga, Daizenshuu, or TV Series.
2) Power Levels which we've guessed based upon the power levels from the Dragon Ball Manga, Daizenshuu, or TV Series.

To formulate our power level guesses, we rely upon the knowledge we've gathered about Dragon Ball over the years. Each guess takes into consideration a characters training, who he/she can defeat at the moment, the known levels of other characters he/she faces, and the overall upward trend in power levels as the series progresses.

How This Chart Works:
Its quite simple really. All of the power levels have been split up into sagas, with the Saiya-jin and Furiza Sagas being subdivided into individual battles (note: these battles are not meant to match those found in our Battle Chart). Along the right column of the chart you will find an assortment of screenshots, captions, and explinations of any power level guesses we've made. Finally, to make heads or tails of the coloration of the power levels, see the Color Key below.

Read Before E-mailing Me About These Levels:
The colored levels are fact as stated in the Japanese Manga, Daizenshuu, and TV Series, therefore they cannot be disputed. I can't even begin to count the number of e-mails I've recieved arguing that the power levels that Akira Toriyama created are wrong! Come on people, the purpose of this section if to document the power levels he wrote for the Japanese Manga, Daizenshuu, and TV Series.

As for the white levels; they are my opinion, for whatever its worth. Simply my 2 cents. However, all of my guesses are based strongly around the stated levels.

Dragon Ball GT:
There never were any power levels given in DBGT, nor was an official list ever created. Therefore there is absolutly no fact upon which to base DBGT power levels. Besides that, DBGT wasn't created or written by Akira Toriyama, so any nuances which he put into Dragon Ball, such as power levels, would have no precedent in DBGT!

A Word on Reference:
Some people have e-mailed me about a "Dragon Ball Guide Book" which supposedly lists levels for the entire series. Let me set the record straight on this one: those levels mean nothing. Come on people! Even those of you who watch the English version of DBZ know that Furiza has over 1,000,000 in his second form. The guide book lists him at about half that. Now, which do you believe? The manga (written by Akira Toriyama himself), the Daizenshus (approved by Toriyama and produced by the same company who compiled the Dragon Ball manga volumes), and the the TV Series (based directly on Toriyama's manga), or an unofficial guide book written years after Dragon Ball's end by a third party who was trying to cash in on the craze?

Color Key:
Color Based On What It Means
1,000,000 Manga\
Daizenshuu		 These levels came from the Japanese manga or Daizenshuu either by direct reading or calculation based on a direct reading. There is no disputing these power levels, as they were written by the creator of Dragon Ball.
Example: The manga states form 2 Furiza has over 1,000,000; or Vegeta: 18,000 * 10 (ozouru) = 180,000
1,000,000 Calculated While not stated outright in the Japanese manga or daizenshu, these levels can be had by taking levels that did appear in the Japanese manga or daizeshu and combining them with other known facts from the Japanese manga or daizenshu.
Example: Vegeta has 18,000. Ozouru multipies your power by 10. Hence, Ozouru Vegeta has 180,000. (180,000 x 10 = 180,000)
-or-
Example: Goku has 300,000. All levels of Kaioken multiply your powerlevel (except Kaiokem x1). Hence, Goku Kaioken x20 has 6,000,000. (300,000 x 20 = 6,000,000)
1,000,000 Scouter Reading These levels came directly from scouter readings in the Japanese version of Dragon Ball. They are simple fact.
Example: "Hmm.. the scouter only says 5,000."
1,000,000 Stated These levels are stated by characters in the Japanese version of Dragon Ball. They are simple fact.
Example: "Saibamen have 1,200 each"
1,000,000 Guesstimate These levels are based on the Manga\Daizenshuu, Calculated, Scouter Reading, and Stated power levels. These are the only disputable levels on this list, but we have taken the extra time and care to assure that these levels have a basis in sound logic.

ALL LEVELS AFTER THIS POINT ARE A GUESS!
In the mangas, Japanese series, and Daizenshuus, Toriyama NEVER listed or authorized any power levels after the Furiza Saga, as he felt they limited his ability to be flexable and creative with the outcome of battles. If you go to another site claiming to have official levels after this point, they are mistaken. In general, these sites are using information from the "Dragon Ball Guide Book" which was published in 1996. Don't let the book's name fool you however, as the facts put forth in the power levels section of the book in no way corrispond to the facts stated in the series, manga, or daizenshuu.
