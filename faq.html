Basic Questions
What is Dragonball?
Dragonball is based on Chinese legend, with a modern twist. The show focuses on a group of Earthlings who know martial arts and are capable of manipulating energy. It is a good ten years old now, though it has only recently been displayed to a mass audience in America.

When is DB/Z on TV?
Please go to our TV Schedule page for any questions on show times.

What channel number is DB/Z played on?
This is a very silly question, considering that most every cable provider has a different channel number. Just check your TV Guide or the preview channel for station numbers.

Who created DB/Z/GT?
DB was created by Toei Entertainment in conjuncture with Bird Studios, and is based on the mangas of Akira Toriyama. The show was produced in Japan, and is dubbed into English by FUNimation. This is why you will see many spellings of names from the series, as the translation is far from perfect. DBGT was also created by Toei/Bird, but Toriyama was not in on the project.

Series Questions
When will Cartoon Network show new episodes?
The original "Dragon Ball" is currently airing on the Cartoon Network. Depending on how well it does in the ratings, we may or may not see a second season on the station. Fifty new episodes of DBZ began airing September 10th. This is the second to last season, the 6th and final season is expected to air sometime in 2002.

What does FUNimation plan to release in the future?
Cartoon Network has purchased the rest of DBZ and DB, and has expressed interest in DBGT. VHS and DVD are supposed to be released simultaneously from now on. Movies 5 and 6 are expected in 2002, and the remaining seven movies will probably be released here as well somewhere down the line. There's a pretty good chance we'll see DBGT on Cartoon Network after DBZ has aired in it's entirety.

What happens in episode such and such/who is this character/etc?
Please, for the sweet love of god, do not e-mail us about what happens in the series. This is what the story, bios, message board, and this page are for. We will pretty much ignore any of these questions, unless we deem that the query is unanswerable from the info on this site. It takes us about 5 minutes to dig up answers to questions we don't know off the top of our heads. Multiply that by the amount of e-mail we get, and that's a lot of time wasted.

How is the version in the US different from the original?
The American variant is greatly censored and cut, which greatly angered viewers who had seen the original. Roughly 19% on average is cut from every episode from seasons one and two; 53 American shows is the same as 65 of the Japanese version. When FUNimation became the sole producer of the dub, the censorship was toned down a bit. The dialogue, on the other hand, has been butchered significantly. And contrary to popular belief, there is no cussing at all in DB, DBZ, or DBGT. The fansubs tend to take liberties with the episodes and add a "fuck" and a "shit" when they feel it is necessary (Although there are mild insults and taunts, not to mention the occasional flipping of the bird ^_^).

Where can I get the DBZ Daizenshuus (World Guides)?
Currently, one of the few places on the internet that offers it is Galaxy Manga. If you live in a large metropolis like New York City, go to the China Town. If you do a little searching, you will find anime stores that will sell the books.

Why are some things listed as "Anime Only?"
The animated series differs from the manga in several locations. For instance, while the Trunks TV Special does have a manga counterpart, the Bardock Special does not. Hence, anything exclusive to the Bardock Special is considered "anime only." All movie and DBGT-specific characters/places/etc are included in this category (Plus series-only characters like Garlic Jr). Anything that does not appear in the manga (with the exception of the movie comics, which were made after the films were released) is marked with the graphic to the right.

How many episodes of DB/Z/GT are there?
Dragonball: 153
DragonballZ: 291
DragonballGT: 64

What is the proper spelling of a(n) character/attack/place?
Vegeta...Vegita...Bejita...Begita...so many ways to spell the same character. But which one is the right one? There certainly are some names that are completely wrong (Frieza, for instance), but many versions are completely acceptable. You have to understand that these names were first written in the Japanese language, and the transition to an Arabic character language (like English) is not always a smooth one. Check our English/Japanese name guide for a huge list of spellings.

Why did the voices change for season 3?
Here's a real doozy of a question. To avoid turning this into an essay, we'll make it short and sweet. FUNi stopped producing episodes for a while when Saban closed their syndication branch. By the time they started back up, Ocean Group (the old studio) was booked with other projects and wasn't ready to start new DBZ eps right away. FUNi figured that with the time it would take to start with Ocean again they could start their own studio, have more creative control over the show, do it for cheaper, and pump out episodes way faster that way. The choice was pretty obvious. To see the cast lists, please visit our voices section.

Is there another DB series besides Draognball, Dragonball Z, and Dragonball GT?
No. Nothing is in production, and nothing is planned. There is no Dragonball AF. Most rumors of a new series come from fan manga drawn in Japan, otherwise known as "doujinshi." Generally, these comics are very well-drawn, and can easily be mistaken for Toriyama's work. However, they are fan-made, and are not considered a part of the DB universe.

Which came first: The manga or the anime?
Dragonball appeared in Jump long before Goku made his debut on television. It has always been several months ahead of the anime (if not more). DBGT is the opposite, the TV show came before the comic (That manga was authored by Toei, not Toriyama).

Who reached SSJ such-and-such first?
Please check out our Saiya-jin Genealogy section for the answer to that question.

What do the 'Z' and 'GT' stand for?
Ah, the eternal question. I'll start with GT since it actually means something: It's an acronym for "Great Touring." The beginning of GT involves a lot of traveling, so it's easy to see where it got its name. Z, on the other hand, stands for nothing. It's just a way to separate the series from Dragonball on television (There was no DBZ manga...it was Dragonball all the way to Ubuu's arrival). And to prove it, I'm going to debunk some supposed meanings of the letter Z: It does not come from "Z Warriors," it is not a reference to the Zet sword, and it is not short for "Zetta," "Zeto (The letter Z is pronounced as zeto in Japan rather than our pronunciation of zee, but zeto does not hold any meaning on its own)," "Zen," or any particular word.

Site Questions
What do you use to create your site?
We use Adobe Photoshop 5.5 and Graphic Converter for the images, and Dreamweaver for the HTML (Pagespinner became tedious). Mr.E owns a G3/450 (Mac), SkullMac has a G4/350, and Kaio owns a dual G4/500 (Lucky bastard). The site is hosted on NYI.net (Celeron 700). The banner at the top of the page covers hosting costs and provides us with some extra cash to purchase stuff we need for the site (equipment, merchandise, etc).

Why isn't file (insert filename here) showing up properly?
95% of the time your browser is not set up properly. If you have not assigned a program to read mov files, when you try to view a clip it will look like garbled text. The simple fix is to install the FULL version Quicktime 5.0, which is available at http://quicktime.apple.com. Don't try to view the movie clips with media player once you have installed QT5, as it will not work. You HAVE to use the quicktime plugin or movie player. Another common problem is that people click on a link and it looks like absolutely nothing happens. In actuality, the file was probably downloaded to your hard drive, and all you need to do is find out where the default download directory is. However, we will occasionally make mistakes with our links. In this case you will see the error page, which will give a 404 and state that the file was not found. If this happens to you, send the proper staff member an e-mail and tell us EXACTLY where the broken link is. We will try to fix it ASAP.

Can you help design my site or give me a few pointers?
We get way too many e-mails on this subject. There are plenty of web design sites out there where you can get some help. Do not e-mail us about this, we will just ignore it.

May I link to your page?
Of course you may, we don't mind the extra hits (Don't even try linking directly to our files, we have anti-leech software installed ^_-).

Can you link to my page please?
Most of the time, the answer is no. However, once every couple months we have a link submission period where we take URLs for a few hours and make a mass update to that section. Since this is a time-consuming process, we have to find a good day to do it, and there will be little warning. If you do miss a submission period, you'll have to wait for the next one. You will see an announcement in the news when we are accepting submissions. We do not add links in the interim.

Please read our how to submit fanart and how to submit fanfic sections for the answer to that question.

How come your site is so popular?
Original content is the key, and updating almost every day. When we first moved to the domain we submitted the site to about half a dozen search engines, and asked a few of our friends to link to us. The popularity grew afterwards on its own (No, Mr.E did not sell his soul to Satan, Celene Dion, or any other being of pure evil).

No...if we shouted out to everyone's "homies" and wished every single visitor a happy birthday, there would not be time for much else, would there? Unless you have some important news that will affect the community, we don't want to hear it.

We make small changes to our layout all the time. Presentation is important, but it is not the heart of a site.

*Makes money sign with fingers* Bandwidth doesn't come cheap, people. We try to give as many people as possible a chance to download without forcing yours truly to dine on rusty nails and shoe leather. And with our current money situtation, that limit is 0. ^_^

Will you post full episodes?
No. If we posted full episodes, the site would use hoards of bandwidth, FUNimation would lose sales, and we could get sued. The risks are too great for us just so you can break the law.

While I am not familiar with all methods of playing Japanese games on American systems, I have taken the time to write out the most popular methods for playing them on American Gameboy, Playstation, Saturn, Super Nintendo, and Turbo Grafx 16 systems.

We prefer the fighters over the RPGs. Don't get us wrong, we love the RPGs, but the DBZ RPGs are... errr... not very challenging. Dragon Power (Shenron no Nazo) - NES
Dragonball Z: The Game (Buyuu Retsuden) - Genesis
Dragonball GT Final Bout (Dragonball Final Bout) - PSX

These games are no longer in production, and are impossible to find new. Check your local used game store, e-bay (be careful what you buy there!), or our list of DBZ Game Retailers.

Will there ever be another DBZ game released in America?
Yes (Insert cheering here)! Infogrames has confirmed that they are developing Dragonball Z games for Gameboy Color and PSX or PS 2 (at least according to IGN) and possibly other platforms. From what we have gathered, you can expect the PS 2 game to totally kick ass (not to mention the fact that they will be listening to fan input and try their very best to please hard-core fans). Details will trickle in over the next year, so expect this Q&A to be updated again.

What's this I see about a French/Korean/British release of a game?
Several of the DBZ games were released in countries other than Japan and America. Some of these games were completely translated into a foreign language, while others were simply sold in other countries without being translated.

I heard there were some DBZ arcade games. If so, why aren't they on your list?
Yes. In fact, they are now included in our ever growing games list. However, as it would be rather hard for us to get our hands on one of these machines, we still rely on our visitors to provide us with information on these games.

Chat Questions
What do I need to use the chatroom?
The basic way to enter our channel is to use the java client in our chat section, which requires simply a java compatible browser. However, the channel can also be accessed with any IRC client by connecting to irc.homelan.com and joining #namek. If you are somewhat techno-savy, I would use the second option.

What are the rules of #namek?
Read the rules right here. All java users are forced to go through this page before they can enter the web-based chat, so ignoring them is inexcusable.

What is the purpose of the room?
The purpose of our chatroom is for DB fans to meet and discuss their lives and animation in general. It is no way restricted to DB chat, as we talk about everything. :) The room is NOT for getting a quick response from the web masters of Planet Namek, use our e-mail for that.

Message Board Questions
Does it cost anything to use the message board and do I need an account to use it?
The message board is totally free. You do, however, need to sign up for an account. This is mostly a security measure on our part.

Will you sell/give away my e-mail address (how private is the message board)?
No. In fact, you can choose to hide your e-mail address from fellow members when you sign up for an account. Other than your e-mail (which can be hidden) you are not required to give away any personal information upon signing up for an account. Unfortunately, due to several attacks we've experienced on the board, I've been forced to turn on the public viewing of IP addresses when someone posts. This does have its advantages outside of administrative use though (you can use them to easily spot an impostor for example).

What are the general rules of the message board?
In general, be respectful, watch the language, no porn, no spam, and keep to the topic of the forum you're posting in. Though these seem like no-brainer rules, I've been forced through numerous encounters to turn them into a multi-paged monstrosity.

I signed up for an account and I haven't received my password via e-mail. What gives?
First, you need to make sure you entered the correct e-mail address when you registered. To do so, search our member database for the username you choose. If you're e-mail is entered correctly, then the problem is most likely on your ISP's end. More often than not, those having problems receiving their password are on AOL and they (or their parents) have set AOL to reject e-mail from those not on AOL. Occasionally, our server becomes overloaded and passwords are simply not sent out. If you are sure this is the case, please wait an hour or two and use this form to have your password sent again. If, after several attempts, the form fails to send your password there is nothing more we can do.
